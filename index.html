Gaming Time -- Know the Facts

Gaming is earned, not given. Here is what happens to your body and brain when you game too much:

What Too Much Screen Time Does

  • Slows your reflexes on the pitch. Your brain gets trained to react to a screen, not a real ball. Real sport needs real-world reaction speed.
  • Kills your sleep. Blue light from screens before bed blocks melatonin. Less deep sleep = less growth hormone = less height.
  • Shrinks your attention span. Games give you dopamine hits every few seconds. Real soccer requires 20+ minutes of sustained focus. Gaming trains the opposite.
  • Weakens your eyes. Hours of close-up screen focus strains your vision. Wingers need sharp long-distance vision to read the pitch.
  • Makes you sit when you should move. Every hour on Roblox is an hour you could be juggling, sprinting, or getting better. Time does not come back.
  • Changes your posture. Hunched over a screen compresses your spine. Bad posture = lost centimeters of height and slower running form.

The rule: Earn your screen time with points. No points = no screens. Gaming is the reward, never the default.
